  • DRUG MANAGEMENT

    A Drug Control Management System (DCMS) is designed to track the lifecycle of pharmaceuticals within a healthcare facility, including procurement, storage, prescription, dispensation, and administration. The system ensures that drugs are used responsibly and legally, preventing misuse and managing inventory efficiently..

    Key Features:
    User Authentication: Different access levels for administrators, pharmacists, doctors, and nurses. Secure login system to ensure only authorized personnel can access and modify data.
    Drug Inventory Management: Track the quantities of each drug in stock. Alerts for low stock levels to prompt reordering. Record details of drug batches, including expiry dates.
    Prescription Management: Doctors can prescribe drugs to patients. Electronic prescription records to track who prescribed what and when. Prevent over-prescription or conflicting prescriptions.
    Dispensation and Administration Tracking: Pharmacists can log the dispensation of drugs. Nurses can log the administration of drugs to patients. Real-time updates to the inventory based on dispensation and administration records.
    Compliance and Reporting: Ensure compliance with legal standards and regulations. Generate reports for audits and inspections. Track the complete history of each drug from procurement to administration.
    Alerts and Notifications: Notify relevant personnel about drug expirations, recalls, or low stock. Alert doctors or pharmacists about potential drug interactions.
